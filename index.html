01 — Executive Summary

Beyond Customer Inquiries,
Not Just Questions.

Of the 7,045 tickets logged between September and November 2025, 6,530 are complaints — a 92.7% complaint ratio that signals systemic upstream failures rather than isolated support issues.

Product-related issues drive 58.2% of all volume: missing items (29.7%), wrong items (13.2%), and quality defects (11.8%). These are warehouse and logistics problems arriving in the support queue. Meanwhile, California operates at a breach rate 4× the national average, and half of all customer survey responses are untagged — crippling root-cause analysis.

The October paradox crystallises the tension: agents achieved a record 96% SLA rate while customer satisfaction fell to its lowest point at 61% CSAT. Speed without quality is not service.

⚠
October Paradox

96% SLA compliance yet 61% CSAT — agents closing tickets fast but not solving problems.

📊
Data Blindspot

2,597 of 4,641 survey responses (55.9%) are unclassified — analysis is operating with half a dataset.

📍
Geographic Fault Line

California's 27.98% breach rate versus 6.8% national average demands immediate resource reallocation.
